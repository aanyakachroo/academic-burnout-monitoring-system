# Academic Burnout and Study Load Monitoring System

---

## 1. Project Name & Overview

**Project Name:**  
Academic Burnout and Study Load Monitoring System  

**Overview:**  
This project is a web-based system that helps monitor students’ academic workload and identify early signs of burnout. Many students face high academic pressure because of continuous assignments, tests, and deadlines.  

The system allows students to enter their study hours, assignments, test schedules, and stress levels. Based on this information, the system calculates a burnout risk level such as Low, Moderate, or High.  

Faculty members, academic counselors, and parents can view reports and alerts so that students who are under too much academic pressure can get timely support.

This project is being developed as part of a Software Engineering course to understand how real-world problems can be converted into software solutions using requirement analysis, system modeling, and basic agile planning concepts.

---

## 2. Problem it Solves

Students often feel stressed and mentally exhausted because of heavy academic workload. During the semester, multiple assignments, tests, and deadlines sometimes come together, making it difficult for students to manage their time properly.  

Most existing academic systems focus only on attendance and grades. They do not track how much workload a student is actually handling or how stressed a student feels. Because of this, academic burnout often goes unnoticed until it starts affecting performance and mental health.  

This project tries to solve this problem by providing a simple software system that helps track academic workload and gives early warnings when students are under excessive academic pressure.

---

## 3. Target Users (Personas)

**1. Student**  
- Enters study hours, assignments, tests, and stress level  
- Views burnout risk score and recommendations  
- Requests counseling support  

**2. Faculty**  
- Views student academic workload  
- Identifies at-risk students  
- Sends academic advisories  

**3. Academic Counselor**  
- Reviews high-risk students  
- Schedules counseling sessions  
- Adds counseling notes and monitors recovery  

**4. Parent / Guardian**  
- Views student progress summary  
- Receives critical burnout alerts  

**5. Admin**  
- Manages users and roles  
- Configures burnout rules  
- Generates system reports  

---

## 4. Vision Statement

The vision of this project is to create a simple academic monitoring system that helps students manage their workload better and reduces the chances of burnout by providing early warnings and support.

The idea for this project was inspired by the common academic stress faced by students during semesters with multiple assignments, tests, and tight deadlines.

---

## 5. Key Features / Goals

- User registration and login  
- Logging of study hours, assignments, and test schedules  
- Self-reported stress level tracking  
- Burnout risk calculation using simple rules  
- Burnout risk categorization (Low, Moderate, High)  
- Alerts and notifications for students, faculty, parents, and counselors  
- Faculty dashboard to monitor academic workload  
- Counseling support and session tracking  
- Administrative control for rules and reports  

---

## 6. Success Metrics

- Number of students regularly logging workload data  
- Number of burnout alerts generated  
- Number of counseling sessions scheduled  
- Reduction in high-risk burnout cases  
- Feedback from students and faculty  

---

## 7. Assumptions & Constraints

**Assumptions:**  
- Students will honestly and regularly update their academic workload  
- Burnout risk is identified only using academic workload indicators  
- Faculty and counselors will use the system to support students  

**Constraints:**  
- The system does not perform medical or psychological diagnosis  
- Burnout detection is rule-based and not AI-based  
- Initial implementation will be a prototype-level system
